What is Liver Cirrhosis?

Liver cirrhosis is a progressive condition where normal liver tissue is replaced by scar tissue, affecting liver function. It often results from long-term liver damage due to alcohol, hepatitis, or other chronic liver diseases.

Symptoms

  • Fatigue, weakness
  • Yellowing of skin (jaundice)
  • Abdominal swelling
  • Loss of appetite

Causes

  • Chronic alcohol abuse
  • Hepatitis B or C infection
  • Non-alcoholic fatty liver disease
  • Autoimmune liver diseases

Prevention

  • Limit alcohol intake
  • Maintain a healthy weight
  • Vaccinate against hepatitis
  • Routine liver checkups
