Revolutionizing Liver Care with AI

HepaCheck is a machine learning-powered system designed to assist in the early detection of liver cirrhosis using non-invasive clinical and laboratory data.

Problem Statement

Liver cirrhosis is a critical health condition that is often diagnosed at a late stage due to the invasive and time-consuming nature of traditional methods. This delays treatment and worsens outcomes. HepaCheck aims to transform liver disease diagnosis by offering fast, accessible, and non-invasive early prediction using AI.

Use Cases

  • Enable early, non-invasive diagnosis of liver cirrhosis by healthcare professionals
  • Support hospital systems to triage high-risk patients effectively
  • Integrate with telehealth platforms to offer remote liver health monitoring

How It Works

Users input basic clinical values into the prediction form. The system processes this data through a trained machine learning model which evaluates patterns indicative of cirrhosis. Results are displayed instantly with a confidence score, helping users make informed decisions or seek medical advice.

Model Documentation

The liver cirrhosis prediction model is a supervised machine learning classifier trained on clinical data. It takes multiple biomarkers and lifestyle features as input and predicts the likelihood of liver cirrhosis.

Model Type

We use a Random Forest Classifier due to its robustness, high accuracy, and ability to handle missing or imbalanced data.

Features Used

  • Total Bilirubin
  • Direct & Indirect Bilirubin
  • SGOT (AST), SGPT (ALT)
  • Albumin, Globulin, Total Protein
  • Platelet Count, PCV (%)
  • Alcohol consumption (duration & quantity)
  • Age, Diabetes status, Blood Pressure

Training Dataset

The model was trained on a cleaned and preprocessed dataset consisting of anonymized patient records. The dataset was balanced using SMOTE, and features were scaled using StandardScaler.

Limitations

  • This tool is not a replacement for clinical diagnosis.
  • The prediction may vary based on data quality or rare outliers.
  • Medical consultation is strongly recommended for confirmed results.
